Community Guide

Visit Byron, Illinois

Welcome Ogle County Quality of Life Economic Development Tourism Organizations

Byron is a destination worth the drive. Whether you're coming for ByronFest, a round of golf on the Rock River, a weekend of drag racing, or just the charm of a genuine Illinois small town, you'll find more to do — and more reasons to come back — than you expected.

Attractions & activities

Byron Dragway

One of the Midwest's premier drag racing facilities, drawing competitors and fans from across the region all season long. Located along the Rock River at 7287 N. River Rd.

byrondragway.com →

PrairieView Golf Club

A beautiful 18-hole public golf course set against the rolling landscape of northern Illinois. Host of the Chamber's Annual Golf Play Day each August.

prairieviewgolf.com →

Byron Forest Preserve

Acres of natural land with hiking trails, the Rock River, fishing access, and wildlife habitat. A gem for outdoor enthusiasts in every season.

byronforestpreserve.com →

Byron Museum of History

Explore the rich history of Byron and the surrounding Rock River Valley at the Byron Museum, located in the heart of downtown at 110 N. Union St.

byronmuseum.org →

Rock River

The Rock River runs through Byron and offers fishing, kayaking, and scenic views. Riverfront Park provides public access and a beautiful backdrop for community events.

Hairy Cow Brewing Company

Byron's local craft brewery at 450 E. Blackhawk Dr. A great stop for a cold pint and local flavor before or after your Byron adventures.

hairycowbrewing.com →

Annual events

Byron's event calendar gives visitors a reason to come back every season. ByronFest (July) is the crown jewel — a three-day festival drawing thousands from across the region with live music, carnival rides, food vendors, and the famous bags tournament. ChiliFest (October) brings the heat in the fall, and Miracle on 2nd Street transforms downtown into a winter wonderland each December.

Dining in Byron

From Costa's Ristorante and Dos Amigos to the Swedish Pancake House, Sweetbean Cafe, and Hairy Cow Brewing — Byron's dining scene punches well above its weight for a city its size. Check the Business Directory for a full list of local restaurants and cafes.

Directions & lodging

Byron is located along Illinois Route 2 and Route 72, approximately 20 miles west of Rockford. Lodging is available in nearby Rockford, Dixon, and Oregon. For visitors staying in the area, Byron is an easy day trip destination or a convenient base for exploring the Rock River Valley.
